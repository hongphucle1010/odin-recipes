Jamaican Beef Patties

These are a spicy, delicious snack that I first tried in London. They are a lot of work, but they are definitely worth it. You can also fill them with whatever you want, but I like the beef the best.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold water
  • 1/2 cup shortening
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/2 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup dry bread crumbs

Directions

  1. Combine flour and 1 teaspoon salt in a medium bowl. Add water and mix to form a dough. Knead dough until smooth, cover and set aside.
  2. Heat shortening in a large skillet over medium heat. Add beef and onion and cook until beef is evenly brown and onion is soft. Season with curry powder, cayenne pepper, and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Stir in bread crumbs and remove from heat.
  3. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  4. Roll out dough to 1/8 inch thickness. Cut into 5 inch circles. Put a large spoonful of filling on half of each circle. Fold dough over filling and crimp edges to seal. Place on a lightly greased baking sheet.
  5. Bake in preheated oven for 30 minutes, or until golden brown.
